# About the Framework

From Wildfires to Regeneration is a framework for moving from wildfire crisis toward long-term ecological and social regeneration. It brings together landowners, Custodians, local communities, Strategic Partners and Patrons in a shared effort — moving from wildfire risk toward long-term ecological and social regeneration, rather than treating fire response and land regeneration as separate problems.

The Framework's conceptual foundation is the Founding Manuscript, *Dagli Incendi alla Rigenerazione*, retained in this repository as a permanent historical record. From that foundation, a Version 1.0 publication suite has been developed: a founding presentation of the Framework, its capability structure, and materials prepared for governance, patron and strategic-partner audiences. See [Publications]({{ '/publications/' | relative_url }}) for the full suite.

## Who the Framework brings together

- **Founding Global Strategic Partners** — organisations contributing a distinctive, essential capability to the Framework's Global Rapid Response model, such as environmental technologies, early detection, aerial response, communications, construction, energy, water systems, or regenerative agriculture.
- **Regional and National Strategic Partners** — contributors of funding, technology, technical expertise, engineering, operations, research or logistics at a regional or national level.
- **Patrons** — contributors of long-term, legacy, strategic impact or transformational funding.
- **Regional Implementation Partners** — organisations responsible for delivering a deployment within a specific region.
- **Custodians** — the distributed network of stewards present on the land, responsible for surveillance during periods of highest wildfire risk and for ongoing environmental monitoring, biodiversity recording and regenerative work throughout the year.
- **Landowners and local communities** — the parties whose land, knowledge and participation the Framework exists to serve, and without whom no deployment can proceed.

*Source: [Governance Framework]({{ '/governance/en/governance-framework/' | relative_url }}), Section 3.*

## Framework capability structure

The Framework's initial deployment is designed around a network of Strategic Partners, each contributing a distinct capability domain. The [Framework Capability Register]({{ '/publications/en/capability-register/' | relative_url }}) documents these in full; the domains identified for Version 1.0 are:

- Environmental Intelligence
- Early Detection Systems
- Wildfire Drone Systems
- Observation Structures
- Structural Engineering &amp; Steel
- Communications Infrastructure
- Landscape Management &amp; Ecological Regeneration
- Renewable Energy &amp; Off-Grid Power
- Water Systems &amp; Pumping Infrastructure

No single organisation can deliver the Framework independently — collectively, these capability domains provide the foundation required to move from concept to a first operational deployment.

## Governance and continued development

The Framework's governance — how it is directed, how communities and partners participate, how it sustains itself financially, how it manages risk, and how it protects and shares its intellectual property — is set out in the [Governance documentation]({{ '/governance/' | relative_url }}) suite, currently at Version 0.1 (working draft).

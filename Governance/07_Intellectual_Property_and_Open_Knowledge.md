# Intellectual Property and Open Knowledge

**Status:** Version 0.1 Working Draft

## Purpose

To set out the approach of the From Wildfires to Regeneration Framework to intellectual property and open knowledge, recognising that the Framework aims to encourage adoption while preserving consistency and attribution.

## Scope

Covers ownership of the Framework's materials, licensing and open knowledge sharing, attribution requirements, and safeguards intended to support consistent adoption by third parties.

## 1. Introduction and Purpose

The Executive Board Paper places the Framework in the tradition of proven models — the Toyota Production System, the Copenhagen Model, the Heidelberg Approach — valued for their "clearly defined methodology, consistent delivery and wider adoption." The Framework's own ambition is to become such a model. Recognising that this aim depends on encouraging adoption while preserving consistency and attribution, this document sets out how the Framework's intellectual property and open knowledge are approached.

## 2. Intellectual Property Principles

- **Contribution does not confer control.** As set out in the Governance Framework (Document 01, Section 6), this principle extends to intellectual property: an approach to IP should protect the consistency of the Framework's methodology without restricting who may benefit from it.
- **Attribution matters.** The founding authorship of the Framework, recorded in the Founding Manuscript, and the contributions of Strategic Partners, Patrons, Custodians and the local community, should be preserved and recognised as the Framework spreads to new regions and partners.

## 3. Ownership and Attribution

**Placeholder — requires committee decision:** the specific vehicle through which the Framework's intellectual property would be held — for example, the legal entity considered in the Legal Structure Paper (Document 03) — has not yet been agreed.

Attribution to the Framework's name and founding origin should be preserved wherever its methodology or materials are adopted or adapted by a new region or partner, consistent with the Legacy Recognition approach already described in the Patron Invitation for funded capability.

## 4. Open Knowledge and Licensing Approach

The Founding Manuscript's own invitation is explicitly open: it presents the founding proposal "as a working basis," inviting landowners, farmers, professionals, associations and the local community to analyse it critically and improve it. This openness to contribution and refinement is already part of the Framework's founding spirit, even though a formal licensing approach has not yet been chosen.

**Placeholder — requires committee decision:** no formal licence (for example, a specific open-content or open-source licence) has yet been adopted for the Framework's materials or methodology. This should be evaluated once the legal structure (Document 03) is clearer.

## 5. Encouraging Consistent Adoption

Consistency of methodology across deployments is already established as a governance principle (Document 01, Section 2: "consistency with local adaptation"). This section extends that principle to adoption by third parties or new regions.

**Placeholder — requires committee decision:** no criteria have yet been defined for recognising or approving a new regional adoption of the Framework, or for distinguishing an authorised adoption from an unaffiliated use of the Framework's name or methodology.

## 6. Enforcement and Dispute Handling

**Placeholder — requires committee decision:** no mechanism has yet been defined for addressing inconsistent or unauthorised use of the Framework's name or methodology. This is expected to depend on the legal structure considered in Document 03.

## 7. Review and Evolution

This document should be revisited once the legal structure (Document 03) and a licensing approach are decided, and periodically thereafter as adoption of the Framework develops.

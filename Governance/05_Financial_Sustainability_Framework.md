# Financial Sustainability Framework

**Status:** Version 0.1 Working Draft

## Purpose

To set out the principles guiding the long-term financial sustainability of the From Wildfires to Regeneration Framework, including funding, resource allocation and financial stewardship.

## Scope

Covers funding and revenue sources, resource allocation, and financial oversight and sustainability planning. Does not include detailed financial projections or budgets.

## 1. Introduction and Purpose

The Founding Manuscript lists start-up financing and long-term economic sustainability among the principal open questions still to be resolved. This Financial Sustainability Framework opens that discussion, drawing on the funding model already described in the Patron Invitation and the Executive Board Paper, and read alongside the Governance Framework's principles on the relationship between contribution and control (Document 01, Section 6).

## 2. Financial Principles

- **Contribution does not confer control.** Patrons and Strategic Partners fund and resource the Framework; they do not thereby acquire control over its regenerative purpose (Document 01, Section 6).
- **Transparency of funding and use.** The Legacy Recognition approach described in the Patron Invitation — dedicating funded capability in a Patron's name, or in honour of a person of their choosing — is an example of funding that remains publicly traceable to its purpose, rather than opaque.
- **Diversification.** Funding drawn from multiple Patrons (contributing long-term, legacy, strategic impact or transformational funding) and multiple Strategic Partners (contributing funding, technology, expertise, engineering, operations, research or logistics) reduces dependence on any single source.

## 3. Funding and Revenue Sources

The Patron Invitation sets out capability-linked patronage packages, each combining an initial contribution with ongoing annual support — for example, a Thermal Surveillance Drone package (initial patronage approximately €62,000, with approximately €6,000 in annual support), an Early Detection Node, a Fixed Observation Station, a Rapid Response Kit, and a full Observation Station (initial patronage approximately €120,000). It also identifies dedicated operational coordination funding — covering Programme Coordination, Operations Management, Logistics and Operations Coordination, Volunteer Coordination, and Administrative Support.

Strategic Partners, as described in the Executive Board Paper, contribute funding, technology, equipment, technical expertise, engineering, operations, research or logistics, according to their role within the Framework.

## 4. Resource Allocation

**Placeholder — requires committee decision:** no formal methodology for allocating funding across the four operational phases (Prevention, Early Detection, Rapid Response, Regeneration) or across regions has yet been agreed.

## 5. Financial Oversight and Accountability

Financial oversight should draw on the same transparency and accountability commitments set out in the Governance Framework (Document 01, Section 5): funding received and its use should be reported against the outcomes the Framework already commits to measuring.

## 6. Long-Term Sustainability Planning

The Founding Manuscript identifies long-term economic sustainability as unresolved. Patron and Strategic Partner contributions, as currently described, principally support the Framework's start-up and initial operational phase. Whether and how the Framework might develop self-sustaining revenue over time — for example, through outputs of its regenerative agriculture activity — has been raised only as a possibility in the founding materials, not as a decided approach.

**Placeholder — requires committee decision:** the long-term financial sustainability model beyond Patron and Strategic Partner funding has not yet been agreed.

## 7. Review and Evolution

This framework should be reviewed periodically alongside the Governance Framework's accountability and review provisions (Document 01, Sections 5 and 7), informed by the Framework's measured outcomes.

## 8. Appendices

Reserved for the detailed patronage package figures published in the Patron Invitation, and for future financial planning material.

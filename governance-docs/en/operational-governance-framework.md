**Status:** Version 0.1 Working Draft

## Purpose

To set out the operational principles and mechanisms through which the day-to-day activities of the From Wildfires to Regeneration Framework are directed and coordinated.

## Scope

Covers operational roles, coordination between implementing partners, and routine decision-making. Does not cover strategic governance or long-term structural decisions, which are addressed in the Governance Framework.

## 1. Introduction and Purpose

The Executive Board Paper describes the Framework's Global Rapid Response model as operating across four phases — Prevention, Early Detection, Rapid Response and Regeneration. This Operational Governance Framework addresses how those phases are directed and coordinated day to day, within the strategic and accountability structure set out in the Governance Framework (Document 01) and alongside the community stewardship roles set out in Document 02.

## 2. Operational Principles

- **Technology supports people; it does not replace them.** The Founding Manuscript states this directly: operational technology extends the observation, verification and coordination capability of Custodians rather than substituting for their presence.
- **Multi-purpose intervention.** Consistent with the Framework's founding design principle, operational activity should be planned to serve more than one purpose wherever practical — for example, infrastructure built for fire prevention should also contribute to water retention, soil regeneration and biodiversity.
- **Progressive, realistic scaling.** The Founding Manuscript is explicit that the Custodian network and its supporting infrastructure should be planned from the realities of the land — visibility, terrain, vegetation, prevailing winds, access — rather than from theoretical targets, and should grow progressively as resources allow.

## 3. Roles and Responsibilities

- **Custodians** carry out fire surveillance during the period of highest wildfire risk, and environmental monitoring, biodiversity recording and regeneration work for the remainder of the year (Document 02).
- **Regional Implementation Partners** are responsible for delivering a deployment within a specific region (Document 01).
- **Strategic Partners** contribute the specialist engineering, technology and operational capability required to establish and maintain a deployment (Document 01; Framework Capability Register).
- **Operational coordination functions** — identified in the Patron Invitation as Programme Coordination, Operations Management, Logistics and Operations Coordination, Volunteer Coordination, and Administrative Support — provide the day-to-day coordination that turns equipment, people and resources into a functioning capability.

## 4. Operational Decision-Making

Day-to-day operational decisions within a deployment should be made locally, by those closest to the conditions on the ground, consistent with the subsidiarity principle set out in the Governance Framework. Decisions affecting the Framework's consistency, methodology or wider adoption are reserved for governance at the founding level (Document 01, Section 4) and are not addressed here.

**Placeholder — requires committee decision:** the specific operational procedures and escalation paths within a deployment have not yet been defined. The Founding Manuscript lists "operational modalities" as an open next step.

## 5. Coordination with Partners and Stakeholders

A single deployment draws on multiple, distinct specialist capabilities — environmental intelligence, early detection, aerial response, structural and observation infrastructure, communications, landscape regeneration, energy, water systems, and mapping and coordination platforms, among others (Framework Capability Register). Operational governance should ensure these contributions function as one integrated system rather than as independent, uncoordinated inputs.

## 6. Performance and Accountability

Operational performance should be tracked against the same outcomes the Framework already commits to measuring — including area protected and regenerated, Custodian network coverage, and community participation — consistent with the Governance Framework's accountability principles (Document 01, Section 5).

## 7. Review and Evolution

**Placeholder — requires committee decision:** detailed operational procedures are expected to be developed and refined as the first deployment progresses and produces operational evidence. No formal review cadence has yet been agreed.
